## Behavioral Stories Format (STAR)

**S** — Situation (context)
**T** — Task (your responsibility)
**A** — Action (what you did)
**R** — Result (outcome, metrics)

Keep stories crisp (60-90 sec spoken). One story can answer multiple questions.

## Suggested Behavioral Stories To Prepare

1. **Conflict with teammate** — disagreement on technical approach, how you resolved
2. **Failed project** — what went wrong, what you learned
3. **Performance issue** — bug you debugged, root cause, fix
4. **Mentoring someone** — junior dev you helped, growth shown
5. **Big technical decision** — trade-off analysis, what you chose
6. **Tight deadline** — how you prioritized, scope management
7. **Cross-team collaboration** — working with non-eng (PM, design, support)
8. **Refactoring legacy code** — approach to large rewrite
9. **Production incident** — outage you handled, post-mortem
10. **Saying no** — pushing back on bad requirements

## Company-Specific Prep

Each company file should contain:
- Recent interview questions (from leetcode/glassdoor/blind)
- Their specific tech stack (matches your strengths?)
- Notable architecture decisions they've blogged about
- Engineering culture notes
- Salary band research

## Update Cadence

- After each weekly lesson batch (~7 days), extract recurring patterns into reusable templates
- After every real interview: dump fresh questions here for future review
